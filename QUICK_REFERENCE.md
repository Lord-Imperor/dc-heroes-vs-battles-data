## Power Fluctuation Notes

### Variable Characters

These characters have significant power variations:

**Superman**: Low 5-B to 2-C
- Base: Low 5-B (Small Planet)
- Peak: 2-C (Low Multiverse)  
- Sun-dipped: Higher 2-C
- Varies based on solar energy

**Wonder Woman**: Low 2-C to 2-C
- Pre-Clayface: Low 2-C likely 2-C
- Post-Clayface: Solid 2-C

**Batman**: 9-A to 2-C
- Base: 9-A (Small Building)
- With prep: Up to 2-C (special armors)

**The Flash**: Variable
- Casual: 4-B to 3-C
- With Speed Force: 2-C+
- Depends on writer consistency

## Stat Abbreviations

### Speed Tiers (Common)
- **Subsonic**: Below speed of sound
- **Supersonic**: Mach 1.1 - 5
- **Hypersonic**: Mach 5 - 10
- **High Hypersonic**: Mach 10 - 25
- **Massively Hypersonic**: Mach 100+
- **Sub-Relativistic**: 1-50% speed of light
- **Relativistic**: 50-100% speed of light
- **FTL**: Faster than light (1-10c)
- **FTL+**: 10-100c
- **Massively FTL**: 100-1000c
- **Massively FTL+**: 1000c+

### Lifting Strength Classes
- **Below Average**: Under 50 kg
- **Regular Human**: 50-80 kg
- **Athletic Human**: 80-120 kg
- **Peak Human**: 120-200 kg
- **Class 1**: 200 kg - 1 ton
- **Class 5**: 1-5 tons
- **Class 10**: 5-10 tons
- **Class 25**: 10-25 tons
- **Class 50**: 25-50 tons
- **Class 100**: 50-100 tons
- **Class K**: 100-1000 tons
- **Class M**: 1000-100,000 tons
- **Class G**: 100,000+ tons
- **Class T**: 1 trillion kg+
- **Class P**: 10^15 kg+
- **Pre-Stellar**: 10^25 kg+
- **Stellar**: Can lift stars
- **Multi-Stellar**: Can lift multiple stars

### Intelligence Classifications
- **Below Average**: Self-explanatory
- **Average**: Normal human intelligence
- **Above Average**: Smarter than average
- **Gifted**: Natural talent and skill
- **Genius**: Exceptional intellectual ability
- **Extraordinary Genius**: World-class intellect
- **Supergenius**: Beyond normal genius (cosmic knowledge)
- **Nigh-Omniscient**: Near-unlimited knowledge
- **Omniscient**: All-knowing

## Character Version Priority

### When Multiple Versions Exist:

1. **Post-Crisis / Post-Flashpoint** (most common reference)
2. **New 52 / Rebirth** (modern continuity)
3. **Pre-Crisis** (historical/legacy reference)
4. **Golden Age** (origin reference)
5. **Alternate universes** (note as variant)

### Document All Major Versions:

**Example: Superman**
- Golden Age (Earth-Two): 4-C
- Pre-Crisis: Varies (5-A to 1-A+)
- Post-Crisis: Varies (Low 5-B to 2-C)  
- New 52: Varies (4-B to 2-C)
- Rebirth: Varies (similar to Post-Crisis)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Don't conflate versions**: Post-Crisis Batman ≠ Dark Knight Returns Batman
2. **Note prep time**: Batman with prep is 2-C, base is 9-A
3. **Consider context**: Some feats are outliers
4. **Power fluctuations**: Many characters vary significantly
5. **Multipliers don't stack linearly**: 2x power ≠ 2x tier

## Questions & Edge Cases

**Q: Character has no VS Battles page - what do I do?**  
A: Compare to similar characters with pages. Use LLM with context from completed heroes.

**Q: Character varies wildly between comics?**  
A: Use "Varies" tier with range (e.g., "Varies, Low 5-B to 2-C")

**Q: Preparation time significantly changes tier?**  
A: Note base tier and "up to [X] with preparation"

**Q: Multiple forms/transformations?**  
A: List all forms with separate tiers (see Superman example)

**Q: Controversial/debated tier?**  
A: Use most commonly accepted tier, note controversy in "notes" field
